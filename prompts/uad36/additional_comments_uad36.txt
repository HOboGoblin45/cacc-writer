You are writing the ADDITIONAL COMMENTS section of a UAD 3.6 URAR.

This section provides structured commentary on scope of work, extraordinary assumptions, hypothetical conditions, prior services, and any other relevant matters affecting the appraisal.

REQUIRED CONTENT (as applicable):

1. Scope of Work Statement
   - Define the extent of research and analysis performed
   - Types of inspection(s) conducted: interior/exterior only, full inspection, no inspection?
   - Extent of comparable market research: how many sales analyzed, time period, geographic area?
   - Methods and techniques employed: direct sales comparison, cost approach, income capitalization
   - Data collection: MLS searches, public records, appraiser's files, other sources
   - Example: "The appraiser conducted a full interior and exterior inspection of the subject property on [date]. Comparable market research included analysis of [number] recent sales within [area] from [date range]. Market data was obtained from [sources: MLS, public records, property managers, etc.]. All three appraisal approaches were analyzed."

2. Limitations of Appraisal
   - Scope limitations (if any)
   - Example: "No interior inspection was performed due to tenant occupancy."
   - Example: "Access to basement was limited due to water heater placement."
   - Example: "Limited comparable data available for properties of this specific type in this market."
   - Example: "Rapid market change may affect the reliability of this appraisal if significant time passes before closing."

3. Extraordinary Assumptions (if applicable)
   - An extraordinary assumption is something that the appraiser assumes to be factual that is not known and is not typical for the market
   - Format: "It is assumed that [specific extraordinary assumption]. If this assumption is not correct, the value conclusion may be significantly affected."
   - Examples:
     * "It is assumed that the subject property is free and clear of liens and encumbrances, subject to the easement noted herein."
     * "It is assumed that the property has no environmental contamination. If hazardous materials are subsequently discovered, the value conclusion may be materially affected."
     * "It is assumed that the subject property will be in [condition state] at the time of closing, as inspected on [date]."
     * "It is assumed that all municipal code violations noted by the local building inspector will be remedied prior to closing at a cost estimated at $[amount]."
   - If NO extraordinary assumptions: "There are no extraordinary assumptions for this appraisal."

4. Hypothetical Conditions (if applicable)
   - A hypothetical condition is something contrary to known fact that the appraiser is instructed to assume
   - Format: "This appraisal is prepared subject to the hypothetical condition that [specific hypothetical condition]. If this condition is not met, the value conclusion may change."
   - Examples:
     * "This appraisal assumes the property is free of serious structural defects, despite the minor settlement observed on [date]."
     * "This appraisal assumes completion of proposed renovations to [description] at an estimated cost of $[amount]."
     * "This appraisal assumes zoning will be changed from [current zoning] to [proposed zoning] as requested in pending application."
   - If NO hypothetical conditions: "There are no hypothetical conditions for this appraisal."

5. Prior Services Disclosure (if applicable)
   - Disclose if the appraiser has previously appraised this property, provided other services, or has a relationship with the parties
   - Format: "The appraiser prepared an appraisal of the subject property on [date] with a concluded value of $[amount]. This current appraisal represents [continuation of that value/material change in conclusion due to market/condition changes]."
   - Or: "The appraiser has provided [other services] for [client/property] within the past [time period]."
   - Or: "The appraiser has no prior services or relationships affecting independence for this assignment."

6. Certification and Independence
   - Brief statement of appraiser's certification status and compliance with USPAP
   - Example: "The appraiser is a Certified Residential Appraiser licensed in [state] and subject to the Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice (USPAP). The appraiser confirms independence from the client, lender, and transaction parties."

7. Market Trends and Forecast (if relevant)
   - Brief commentary on market direction if it affects interpretation of appraisal results
   - Example: "The subject market has experienced [rate]% appreciation annually over the past [period]. Current market indicators suggest [stable/improving/declining] conditions ahead."

8. Extraordinary Market Conditions
   - Note if appraisal prepared during extraordinary market conditions (pandemic, recession, natural disaster, etc.)
   - Impact on data reliability and comparable availability
   - Example: "This appraisal is prepared during the [2024] [description of market condition]. Limited comparable data was available due to [market constraint]. The concluded value represents the most probable price under current conditions but may be subject to volatility as market conditions normalize."

9. Appraiser's Comments on Property and Value
   - Final summary comments or notable observations
   - Any factors that make this property unusual or particularly noteworthy
   - Example: "The subject property is exceptionally well-maintained and represents good value in the current market. Comparable properties with similar characteristics command similar prices."
   - Example: "The property's location near [feature] adds premium value. Market evidence supports a [X]% value premium for this micro-location advantage."

10. Special Conditions or Considerations
    - Zoning non-conformity (if applicable) and impact on value
    - Code violations or repairs needed (if applicable)
    - Environmental factors (proximity to landfill, power lines, flood zone)
    - Example: "The property is located within the [flood zone designation] per FEMA Flood Insurance Rate Maps. This may affect flood insurance requirements for the borrower."
    - Example: "Deferred maintenance noted: [item], estimated repair cost $[amount]. This has been factored into the condition rating and value conclusion."

STRUCTURE AND TONE:
- Address each category as applicable to the specific appraisal
- Keep comments concise but complete
- Use professional appraisal language
- Be factual and avoid speculation
- If a category does not apply, state clearly (e.g., "There are no extraordinary assumptions for this appraisal.")

CRITICAL RULES:
- Extraordinary assumptions and hypothetical conditions MUST be prominently noted if they materially affect value
- Prior services disclosures are REQUIRED if applicable
- Do NOT minimize the impact of extraordinary assumptions or hypothetical conditions
- Keep total section to 6-10 paragraphs
- Professional, authoritative appraiser voice
