You are writing the SALES COMPARISON APPROACH section of a UAD 3.6 URAR.

This section comprises approximately 67 pages of the complete appraisal report and includes detailed narratives for each comparable property with specific dollar adjustments and market data support.

SECTION STRUCTURE:
This narrative must include:
1. Introduction and Comparable Selection Methodology
2. Market Time Adjustment explanation
3. Per-Comparable Micro-Narratives (for each of 3 comparables)
4. Adjustment Summary and Analysis
5. Reconciliation of Comparable Indications

REQUIRED CONTENT FOR EACH COMPARABLE:

Per-Comparable Micro-Narratives (must include for each comp):
- Property Address: Specific street address
- Sale Price: Exact sale price, date of sale, days on market
- Proximity to Subject: Distance and area context (same neighborhood or extended search)
- Location Characteristics: How location compares to subject (better/worse/similar)
  - Road access, proximity to amenities, school district, employment centers
  - Location rating comparison (Urban/Suburban/Rural match to subject)

- Site Characteristics: Lot size, topography, utilities comparison
  - Does comparable have similar utilities?
  - Is site size similar? (e.g., "Comparable 1 has a slightly smaller lot of 12,000 SF vs. subject's 15,000 SF")

- Structural/Design Characteristics:
  - Design style: UAD code (e.g., DT, CL, CT) and style match to subject
  - Construction type and condition rating (C1-C6)
  - Quality rating (Q1-Q6)
  - Year built and effective age — compare to subject's effective age

- Property Features:
  - GLA (gross living area) and comparison to subject
  - Bedroom/bathroom count and match to subject
  - Basement: type, finish, square footage
  - Garage: type, capacity
  - View rating (N/B/A) and comparison to subject

- Condition Assessment: Physical condition rating using UAD C1-C6 scale
  - Does comparable appear to be in similar condition to subject?
  - Any notable repairs or maintenance issues affecting value?

CRITICAL ADJUSTMENT RULES:
- EVERY adjustment must be a DOLLAR AMOUNT, not a percentage
- EVERY adjustment must cite the basis: paired sales, income capitalization, cost depreciation, or market research
- Show the math: adjustment per SF x SF difference = total, or comparable price - subject cost = adjustment value
- Quantify market time adjustment using market data from comparable sales (e.g., "Market data indicates an appreciation rate of 3.2% annually, or approximately 0.27% per month. Comparable sold 45 days ago; time adjustment = $[amount]")
- Do NOT invent adjustment amounts

NET AND GROSS ADJUSTMENT ANALYSIS:
- Calculate Net Adjustment: sum of all increases minus sum of all decreases
- Calculate Gross Adjustment: sum of absolute values
- Gross Adjustment % = Total Gross Adjustment / Comparable Sale Price
- Standard guideline: Gross adjustments should not exceed 15-20% of comparable sale price
- Example: "Comparable 1: Net adjustment of $28,000 (1.5% of sale price) and gross adjustments of $125,000 (6.8% of sale price), both within acceptable limits."

BRACKETING AND RANGE ANALYSIS:
- Verify that the subject's key characteristics fall within the range of the comparables
- Example: "The subject's GLA of 2,500 SF falls between Comparable 2 (2,200 SF) and Comparable 1 (2,750 SF), establishing a bracket. The subject's condition rating (C3) also falls within the comparable range (C3-C4), supporting the reliability of the approach."

RECONCILIATION TO FINAL INDICATION:
- After all adjustments, each comparable provides an adjusted indication
- Summarize how the three indications converge or diverge
- Explain which comparables received the most weight and why
- Provide reconciliation to a point estimate or range

CRITICAL RULES FOR THIS SECTION:

- Do NOT use vague language like "similar to subject" or "comparable condition"
- Do NOT invent comparable data
